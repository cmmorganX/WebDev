How is Wind Energy used?

In the past, wind energy was used in sailing and milling production. Today, wind energy is useful in many ways. We use turbines to power homes, farms, and businesses. In 2019 alone, wind energy generated nearly 300 billion kilowatthours (kWh) and about 7.3% of the total United States utility scale electricity.

Is wind energy safe for the environment?

In the early days of wind farming, there were complaints about turbine footprints and abuse of eminent domain. There were also concerns over the impact it would have on local wildlife, especially birds. Many landowners were worried about windmills dominating the landscape or taking over lucrative farmland.

Advancements in construction and technology have resolved all of these concerns. Better planning and updated techology lead to smaller blades, slimmer overall design, and a compact footprint. Today, turbines are often built on pastureland without interrupting day to day function. That doubles efficiency!

Early wind farms did have an impact on wildlife population and safety. The close proximity of one turbine to the next made very narrow passageways, interrupting bird migration. The blades themselves caused several bird and bat casualties.

Today we build turbines much further apart allowing for safe passageway. They are also equipped with sensors and will shut down when wildlife is nearby. Future wind farms are sited in less populated areas and away from natural habitats. Ultrasonic speakers are also placed in certain areas to keep bats away from the blades.
