Peacock Facts Overview

Habitat: Freshwater, swamps, marshes, lakes, rivers
Location: USA and China
Lifespan: 35 to 50 years
Size: 10 to 15 feet (American Alligator)
5 to 7 feet (Chinese Alligator)
Weight: 1,000 pounds
Color: Dark gray, black, brown
Diet: Fish, shellfish, birds, small mammals
Predators: Large snakes, fish, turtles, birds, otters, and bobcats prey on juvenile alligators
Top Speed: 35 kph (22mph) in water
No. of Species: 2
Conservation Status: American Alligator : Least concern
Chinese Alligator : Critically endangered
As large reptiles, alligators are cold-blooded and carnivorous. The Chinese alligator lives in certain parts of China, while American alligators roam areas in the southeastern United States and Mexico.
