BLACK TEA

X-Large Modal Example

When black tea oxidizes completely, it yields an amber-colored tea with a robust, rich flavor. In the majority of western nations, this type of tea is the most widely consumed, and it may be found in numerous blends, including Chai, Earl Grey, and English Breakfast.

X-Large Modal Heading

Completely oxidized tea leaves are used to make all black teas. Unlike the strong, astringent tea well-known in the other main black tea-producing nations, Chinese black teas are produced by slowly withering and oxidizing the leaves to provide a softer, more fragrant tea. Tea made from complete loose leaves is also preferred by the Chinese. The tea's flavor is largely influenced by its cultivation and processing environment. We refer to this sense of location as terroir, which is the belief that the final tea is influenced by the soil, rain, sun, moon, and climate.
